Experiment 018 - Classifier stability and capacity validation

Objective:
Repeat file-based holdout validation across multiple seeds to verify whether the compact ensemble result from Experiment 017 is stable.

Dataset:
- Total samples/windows: 417
- Features per sample: 231

Validation:
- File-based stratified holdout: 80/20
- Seeds: [7, 13, 21, 42, 84, 126, 168, 210, 336, 512]
- Fisher Score ranking recomputed using training data only for each seed

Compared models:
- custom_tree_depth6: top_k=126, units=1, max_depth=6
- custom_tree_depth8: top_k=150, units=1, max_depth=8
- gradient_boosting_depth3_5: top_k=231, units=5, max_depth=3
- gradient_boosting_depth3_10: top_k=231, units=10, max_depth=3
- gradient_boosting_depth3_20: top_k=231, units=20, max_depth=3
- gradient_boosting_full_100: top_k=126, units=100, max_depth=3
- extra_trees_100: top_k=180, units=100, max_depth=None

Best mean result:
- classifier: gradient_boosting_depth3_20
- top_k: 231
- estimated model units: 20
- max depth: 3
- accuracy mean: 0.687644
- accuracy std: 0.081081
- macro F1 mean: 0.688165
- macro F1 std: 0.081176
- movement F1 mean: 0.646287
- movement F1 std: 0.088339

Interpretation rule:
If the compact model remains close to or better than larger models on mean macro F1, it is a strong candidate for future embedded implementation. If larger models consistently outperform it, the compact ensemble may still be too simple.
